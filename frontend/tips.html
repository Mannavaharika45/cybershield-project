Cyber Safety Intelligence

Social engineering tactics evolve daily. Use these industry-standard practices to drastically reduce your attack surface.

Enable 2FA Everywhere

Two-factor authentication blocks 99.9% of automated attacks. Use an authenticator app rather than SMS whenever possible.

Check the URL

Typosquatting is common. Always verify the domain name (e.g., paypaI.com instead of paypal.com). Look for HTTPS, though it doesn't guarantee safety.

Beware of Urgency

Scammers create artificial time crunches ("Account will be deleted in 24 hours!"). Pause and verify independently before reacting.

Use Password Managers

Never reuse passwords. A password manager generates and stores complex passwords, and physically cannot enter credentials on a fake site.

Zero Trust Email

Do not trust the "From" address. Emails can be easily spoofed. Do not download unexpected attachments, especially Office docs or ZIPs.

Smishing Awareness

SMS phishing is rising. Banks and government agencies will never ask for your PIN, password, or direct money transfers via text message.
